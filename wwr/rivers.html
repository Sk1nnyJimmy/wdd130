Our Favorite Rivers

Facts about the rivers we run

Green River, UT

The Green River is a Colorado River tributary that starts in Wyoming and flows through Colorado and Utah. The river's course is about 730 miles. It traverses through the Colorado Plataeu, where it carved some beautiful canyons.

Colorado River, UT

The Colorado River is a 1,450 mile-long river that flows through Colorado, Utah, Arizona and touches the Nevada and California state-border. This river also flows through the Colorado Plateau, just like the Green River. It continues on to flow through the Grand Canyon as well.

Snake River, ID

The Snake River is the largest tributary of the Columbia River. It starts in Wyoming and flows through Idaho, Oregon, and Washington. The length of this river is 1,078 miles-long. Native Americans have lived along the Snake for more than 11,000 years.
